Untitled Poetry

Untitled by Robert Kroker

In this world where we value the emotionally charged deluge of stories filled with suffering,
We fear the loss And the guilt of not doing enough.
hope, not fear should prevail here.
for the cure lives in the former though the latter is most clear.

Pain from a distance, when we can.
Control what we choose to feel, it's the easiest way to deal with loss
when we forget to give hope to those who need it to feel.

I live today for the moment,
and the people, and the joy of helping others
to crawl out of despair.
If you want to help someone, ask first, if they care
then in their darkness
for them, be there

Turn around and see all the lives
near me who need to be heard,
who hope to be seen and tell them
"I love you" and "You're worth it to me."
Hold on to a hand longer than
you should. Stress the point that
there is hope for this world
not just this day. Not just
one time, but every moment
of your life think of all the
lives your love can save.
