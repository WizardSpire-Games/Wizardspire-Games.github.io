Pottery has been around longer than the written word. Pottery is the product and process of forming clay and firing it at high tremperature to give objects a hard durable crystaline structure. The Firing process basically turns mud into stone.

Pottery is one of the oldest human inventions, originating before the Neolithic period, with ceramic objects such as the Gravettian culture Venus of Dolní Věstonice figurine discovered in the Czech Republic dating back to 29,000–25,000 BC.[3] However, the earliest known pottery vessels were discovered in Jiangxi, China, which date back to 18,000 BC. So what? If the ancients could create pottery without all the fancy modern technology, we too can learn this process and make our own pottery from scratch. Now that's a funny term... where does clay come from? In the modern day most people get their clay from a store. However, if you want to source your own clay from the world around you you will only need to go outside and look down at your feet. At any given moment you are likely standing on top of some clay since it comes from dirt. Some dirt has more clay than other dirt, but there is clay just about everywhere. Officially clay is just sedement with particles less than 0.00016" so it's really tiny dirt. I would suggest checking out Andy Ward's Ancient Pottery channel on YouTube for his video How To Make Clay At Home (It's Just Dirt) as this subject is beyond the scope of this project. Here we just want to burn it. On that note, we also won't be talking about working or shaping your pottery either, for that go get some clay and play with it. Just make anything. You don't want to practice firing with your masterpiece so we just need something simple. Tiles with simple pressed designs are a good start.

What the heck am I doing? Making a wild pottery kiln. The basic idea is to heat up the pottery pieces with fire. So we will need something to burn. Seasoned hardwood, or lump charcoal is best but if all we have is generic pressed briquettes that will work too. Now we need something to burn it in, like a pit in the ground. we want to dig deep enough to remove all the grass in about a 3 foot (1 meter) diameter circle. then dig down about 1 foot letting the sides slope in at approximately 45%. Next we want to create an insulation layer between the ground and the pottery. some old broken pottery, ceramic plates, bricks, or flat stones will work to lay in the bottom of the pit. Now we can build a ring of wood around the perimeter leaving a space in the middle large enough for our work pieces. gently place our work pieces in the center and carefully fill the circle with twigs and paper to make it easy to light the fire. finally we will need something to keep the heat in, some old rusty sheet metal will do. We need enough to cover most of the fire leaving a hole in the middle for the smoke to come out. Burning time! light the fire, wait until the wood or charcoals have gray ash on their tips and place the metal heat shield on the fire. remember to leave a hole in the middle for the smoke to escape. Note: if you are using Charcoal you should not see much smoke, this is a good option if you live in a poulated area.
